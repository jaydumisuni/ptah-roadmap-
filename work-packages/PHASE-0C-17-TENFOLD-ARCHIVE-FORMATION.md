## Execution rule

A standard formation contains twenty privates and handles at most ten ordinary records through paired primary/verification lanes.

Complexity escalation:

- focused: 20;
- component: 40+;
- subsystem: 60+;
- system: 80–100+;
- complex large: up to 120.

A complex record consumes more force rather than receiving weaker review.

## Evidence and promotion boundary

Archive privates may collect, compare, challenge and retain evidence. They may not:

- accept or reject a donor;
- alter Ptah architecture;
- reopen Phase 0A;
- authorize source reuse;
- authorize runtime implementation;
- publish private source contents.

Archive Officers reconcile evidence. Promotion to `accepted for archive` requires retained primary evidence, independent verification, contradiction review, privacy/rights review and a durable acceptance checkpoint.

`accepted for archive` means the archive record is trustworthy for recovery. It does not mean the source is adopted or its claims become Ptah truth.
